Travel Diaries


The Sun Family Takes on Spain: Pt. 2

12/25/24-12/30/24

Last time we went to Spain we felt like the time was too tight since we went around to three different cities, so this time around Barcelona was our permanent base. I went in with no expectations since my mom usually plans everything anyway and I had been busy with finals in the weeks prior.

Honestly, I think I enjoyed this trip more than the first! In Barcelona we were able to get a taste of the rich Catalonion culture; many locations described the Catalan traditions with evident pride for the region. However, the majority of our time was undoubtedly monopolized by architect Antonio Gaudi--he is a BIG name in the city. Although I didn't know all that much about architecture, the whimsical nature-inspired art nouveau structures were absolutely bewitching. Casa Batlló looked straight out of an undersea fairytale, while the dazzling light shining through the Sagrada Familia's stained glass seemed to light the interior ablaze in the fire of a thousand colors.

On the second to last day, we also took a tour to a few locations outside of the city. The old town and beach were lovely, but Mt. Monserrat was the most magnificent by far; to my surprise, it was somehow just as beautiful on camera! Unfortunately my picture-taking preoccupations led to me losing my shoulder-bag, which we spent the rest of the time there looking for to no avail.

All in all Barcelona was a fantastic experience and I can't wait to come back someday.
